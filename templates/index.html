History


Welcome to DerHuaso, a restaurant that brings the flavors of Chilean dishes to Germany. Our story began in 1995 when our founder, Ignacio, moved to Germany from Chile. He missed the taste of home and wanted to share it with others. So, she started cooking traditional Chilean dishes in her kitchen and invited her friends over to try them. They loved the food so much that they encouraged her to open a restaurant.


We’re proud to say that we use only the freshest ingredients in our dishes and prepare everything from scratch. We also have an extensive selection of Latin American wines and cocktails to complement your meal.


Thank you for choosing DerHuaso Restaurant We hope you enjoy your dining experience with us!



Chilean Food: A Culinary Journey


Chilean cuisine is a reflection of the country’s diverse geography and rich culinary heritage. The coastal region offers an abundance of seafood, including oysters, scallops, salmon, clams, and Patagonian King Crab. Chilean seafood is often prepared simply, allowing the natural flavors to shine through. Traditional dishes like empanadas and pastel de choclo showcase the country’s culinary heritage. The Spanish influence can be seen in fisherman’s stews, while fusion dishes like ceviche and sushi reflect other culinary influences


Chilean Wine: A Perfect Pairing


Chilean wine is another highlight of the country’s gastronomy. The coastal wine regions produce crisp and aromatic wines that pair perfectly with seafood. Sauvignon Blanc from Leyda offers herbal aromatics and citrus fruit flavors, while Chardonnay from Limari is known for its tropical and mineral-rich profile. For red wine lovers, cool climate Pinot Noir from Casablanca Valley is earthy and fruity, making it an excellent choice for seafood pairings.
